Blake Bronson-Bartlett is associate editor at the Mark Twain Papers and Project (UC, Berkeley). He has taught a wide range of courses in US literatures at the University of Iowa and the Technical University of Dortmund, Germany. His research and writings mainly focus on the nineteenth-century US, though his interests in the history of the book and writing tools have led him beyond US borders. He is the creator/editor of Mabbott Poe, a digital resource on the papers of Poe scholar and editor Thomas Ollive Mabbott. He is also co-translator, with poet and artist Robert Fernandez, of Stephane Mallarme's poems and manuscripts and of Felix Morisseau-Leroy's Haitian Creole version of Antigone.
